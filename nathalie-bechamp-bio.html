Nathalie Béchamp

Acting Director General, Innovation and Investment at Global Affairs Canada

Nathalie Béchamp has been a career diplomat since 1995, specializing in economic and commercial diplomacy, including in the areas of trade and investment promotion, and negotiating international trade agreements. She is currently Director General of Innovation and Investment at Global Affairs Canada (GAC) with a mandate to attract foreign direct investment and support greater technology commercialization of Canadian SMEs in global markets.

Up until recently she held the position of Executive Director of the Innovation, Science and Technology Division, and from 2014 to 2016, she was Minister Counselor responsible for Commercial and Economic Affairs at the Canadian Embassy in the Russian Federation with concurrent accreditation to the Republics of Armenia and Uzbekistan.

From 2011 to 2014, she was Director of the Investment Promotion Division and previously she was the Senior Trade Commissioner at the Canadian Consulate General in New York City (2007 – 2011). Previous assignments include as Director of Commercial Relations with Europe and Central Asia, Acting Director of the Team Canada Missions Division, Deputy Chief Negotiator on the Canada-EU Trade Agreement, and postings to Santiago, (Chile), Bangkok, (Thailand), Brasilia (Brazil) and Ankara (Turkey).

Notable achievements:

  • * Series of high-level engagement initiatives, including ministerial Roundtable Discussions at the World Economic Forum (Davos, 2003-04, 2012-14).
  • * Nation Branding Master Class (London, 2013); distinguished investment panelist (Dubai, 2013).
  • * Participant in the Governor General’s Leadership Conference (across Canada, 2008).
  • * Military evacuation of Canadian and US nationals from Indonesia (Jakarta, 1998).

Ms. Béchamp holds a Bachelor of Commerce degree from the University of Ottawa (1993) and is fluent in English, French, Spanish and Russian.

She lives in Ottawa with her two sons.
