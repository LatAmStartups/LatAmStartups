Laura Ortiz Montemayor

Founder, SVX Mexico

Laura Ortiz Montemayor is a social entrepreneur, Founder of SVX Mexico, and an advocate for Impact Investing, Non-extractive Finance and Regenerative Culture. During the past years, Laura's work has focused on driving social, economic, and environmental justice through Impact Investment and Social Entrepreneurship education, and consulting. She is also an active promoter of Sistema-B, a peer reviewer for The Impact, part of the Mexico Impact Investing Steering Committee and Taskforce, Member of Amexcap, Mexican Association of Entrepreneurs ASEM, Nexus, and Blockchain Mexico.

Laura's mission is to make impact investments the rule and not the exception in Mexico and aligning sustainable values with investment decisions. She has taught impact investing and alternative structures for Amexcap, Universidad Anahuac, Mexico Impact Investment Training, ITESM, Ashoka and has been a speaker on the subject at the UN, Social Finance Forum, SOCAP, Latin American Impact Investing Forum, among other events. Her experience in mentoring entrepreneurs ranges from Village Capital, New Ventures, Sensecube, Unreasonable, to Micromentor and Socialab. Previously she worked for over 8 years in wealth and asset management in BBVA Bancomer and Banamex.

Laura has a degree in International Business from ITESM (Tec de Monterrey) and has additional certifications on Impact Investment from Oxford University, Venture Capital and Private Equity Seminar of RiskMathics, Investment Management training by ANDE and Behavioral Economics by NYU.
